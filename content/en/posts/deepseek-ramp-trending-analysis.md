> **DeepSeek topping Ramp Trending signifies that the AI elimination round has begun. This is not propaganda about "China's AI rise," but the result of 50,000 American enterprises voting with real money.**

## 1. Why Did DeepSeek Win?

### 💰 Price: Cutting Costs by 95%

<img src="/tech-blog/images/deepseek/deepseek-price-comparison---14803624-a948-43f7-9afb-53a72dc4e193.png" alt="DeepSeek vs GPT-4o Price Comparison" style="max-width:100%; height:auto; border-radius:8px;">

| Model | Input Price | Output Price |
|---|---|---|
| GPT-4o | $2.5/M tokens | $10/M tokens |
| DeepSeek V3 | **$0.27/M tokens** | **$1.1/M tokens** |

**Conclusion: DeepSeek is 90-95% cheaper than GPT-4o.**

For SMEs, this means one thing: with the same budget, you can call 10-20 times more tokens.

### ⚡ Technology: No Less Than GPT-4o

DeepSeek V3's MoE architecture (671B parameters, 37B activated) matches GPT-4o on benchmarks like MMLU and Math.

R1's reasoning capability is on par with o1, but at a fraction of o1's price.

**Conclusion: It's not "cheap but barely usable," it's "overwhelming cost-performance."**

### 🎯 Positioning: Serving the Clients OpenAI Abandoned

OpenAI's pricing defines it as a large-enterprise company. SMEs:
- No dedicated support
- No bargaining power
- Throttled when exceeding limits

DeepSeek fills this gap.

**Conclusion: DeepSeek won not OpenAI's large clients, but the SMEs OpenAI doesn't bother with.**

## 2. What Does This Mean?

### 🏢 Business: The Flywheel Starts Spinning

The Ramp chart proves: **DeepSeek has crossed from technology to revenue.**

This is not fundraising valuation — fundraising can rely on stories. Revenue must rely on products.

> 50,000+ real payments from American enterprises = market validation success

This means DeepSeek has entered the procurement catalogs of U.S. enterprises. With stable recurring revenue and an enterprise-grade sales channel, the flywheel is now turning.

### ⚔️ Competition: Whose Cheese Is Being Moved?

<img src="/tech-blog/images/deepseek/deepseek-market-position---2f263938-884a-423a-818b-47fb2c5ccaeb.png" alt="AI LLM Market Positioning Map" style="max-width:100%; height:auto; border-radius:8px;">

**OpenAI's SME customers are churning.**

GPT-4 is powerful, but DeepSeek is sufficient and cheap. For scenarios like code generation, customer service bots, and content processing, "GPT-4-level performance" is a luxury, not a necessity.

**Claude eats the large clients, DeepSeek eats the SMEs — that's the current landscape.**

Anthropic topping Fastest Growing shows: the share eroded from OpenAI is mostly eaten by Claude, not DeepSeek.

**Conclusion: DeepSeek and Claude are eating different markets, not in direct competition for now.**

### 🌍 Geopolitics: An Inevitable Surprise

DeepSeek's rise under the background of U.S. chip controls has three possible reasons:
1. Training data was prepared before controls took effect
2. More alternative choices for inference chips
3. The "auditability" of open-source models reduces security concerns

**Conclusion: DeepSeek has entered the core U.S. B2B procurement system — a first for Chinese AI.**
