# Deep Dive Report on MENA Internet Unicorns: The ICT Landscape Behind the Trillion-Dollar Valuation of 6 Companies

**Author: Tech Observer | November 2025**

> This report deeply analyzes the six most representative internet unicorns in the Middle East and North Africa (MENA) region—Careem, Noon, Shahid, Souq/Amazon, Talabat, Yalla—covering financial performance, ICT investment, cloud service architecture, data center strategy, and latest strategic moves. The combined valuation of the six exceeds $15 billion, with businesses spanning ride-hailing, e-commerce, streaming, food delivery, social entertainment, and more.

---

## 1. Careem: Cloud-First Strategy of an Uber-Backed Super App

**Valuation: $3.1 billion (Uber acquisition price) | HQ: Dubai | Coverage: 15 countries, 120 cities**

### Core Findings

- **Super app strategy**: Launched in 2021, integrating ride-hailing, food delivery, payments (Careem Pay), and instant delivery
- **e& strategic investment**: Received a $400 million investment from the UAE telecom group in 2023 to accelerate super app business expansion
- **Multi-cloud architecture**: 800+ microservices, primarily based on AWS + GCP
- **AI-driven**: Fraud detection (AWS Neptune graph database), demand forecasting, personalized recommendations
- **Cloud-first strategy**: No self-built data centers; fully leverages AWS/GCP Middle East (Bahrain, UAE) infrastructure
- **Projected 2025 revenue exceeding $2.8 billion**

### Technical Architecture

```
AWS (Core) + GCP (Big Data Analytics)
└── 800+ Microservices
    ├── AI/ML (GCP BigQuery)
    ├── Payments (Careem Pay)
    └── Logistics Tracking
```

## 2. Noon: The $1 Billion Empire’s Azure Cloud Transformation

**Valuation: $1 billion+ | HQ: Dubai | Coverage: UAE, Saudi Arabia, Egypt**

### Core Findings

- **Founding background**: Emaar founder Alabbar partnered with Saudi Public Investment Fund (PIF), securing an initial $1 billion investment in 2016
- **Business ecosystem**: Noon.com + Noon Food + Noon Minutes (15-minute delivery) + Noon Pay
- **Cloud services**: Noon Payments’ core business has been migrated to Microsoft Azure
- **2025 projected GMV of $5-7 billion, approaching profitability**
- **Dual listing plan**: IPO in Saudi Arabia and UAE within the next 18–24 months
- **Autonomous delivery**: Pilot launched in Abu Dhabi in November 2025

### Financial Highlights

| Metric | Data |
|--------|------|
| Annual GMV | $5–7 billion |
| Growth rate | 30%–50% YoY |
| Target markets | UAE, Saudi Arabia, Egypt |
| ICT spend ratio | 15%–25% of opex |

## 3. Shahid: Cloud Support for MBC Group’s Streaming Ambitions

**Parent: MBC Group (owned by Saudi PIF) | HQ: Dubai | Coverage: 22 Arab countries**

### Core Findings

- **Market position**: The largest Arabic VOD platform in the MENA region
- **Content advantage**: Exclusive rights to all MBC Group broadcast TV content, massive library of native Arabic content
- **Saudi positioning**: Core to the Kingdom’s “Vision 2030” digital entertainment strategy, strongly backed by PIF
- **Multi-cloud strategy**: Primarily uses AWS + CDN acceleration to support large-scale video streaming
- **User scale**: Paid subscriber base continues to grow; Saudi users account for over 40%

## 4. Souq → Amazon.ae: Amazon’s Middle East Gambit

**Parent: Amazon (NASDAQ: AMZN) | Acquisition price: $575 million (2017)**

### Core Findings

- **Historical status**: Souq.com was hailed as the “Amazon of the Middle East.” Founded in 2005, it was one of the region’s earliest e-commerce platforms.
- **Amazon acquisition**: Acquired for $575 million in 2017, subsequently rebranded as Amazon.ae/.sa/.eg
- **Technology integration**: Fully integrated with AWS, leveraging Amazon’s tech stack to upgrade logistics, payments, and recommendation systems
- **FBA (Fulfillment by Amazon)**: Introduced Amazon’s mature fulfillment system, significantly improving the delivery experience
- **AWS Middle East infrastructure**: Amazon.ae deeply relies on the AWS Middle East (Bahrain) Region

### Technical Advantages

```
Amazon technology empowerment:
├── AWS (Cloud Infrastructure)
├── DynamoDB (Database)
├── SageMaker (AI Recommendations)
└── Logistics Network (FBA)
```

## 5. Talabat: The Tech Backbone of the Gulf Food Delivery War

**Parent: Delivery Hero (listed in Frankfurt) | HQ: Kuwait | Coverage: 6 countries**

### Core Findings

- **Delivery Hero subsidiary**: Acquired by European food delivery giant Delivery Hero in 2020
- **Tech stack**: Primarily built on AWS; microservices architecture supporting high-concurrency order processing
- **Market coverage**: Kuwait, Saudi Arabia, UAE, Qatar, Bahrain, Oman
- **Rider network**: Operates over 100,000 riders across the six GCC states
- **Rapid expansion**: Consolidated local food delivery platforms through M&A, forming a Gulf food delivery monopoly

## 6. Yalla: The Technology Driving a Profitability Miracle

**NYSE: YALA | HQ: Dubai | Listed in 2020 (the first MENA unicorn to IPO on a US exchange)**

### Core Findings

- **Unique business model**: Voice-centric social (Yalla) + social gaming (Yalla Ludo), focused exclusively on the Arab market
- **Astonishing profitability**: Net profit margin consistently above 40%; 2024 revenue exceeded $300 million
- **Asset-light technology**: Primarily built on AWS, with no need for heavy-asset logistics infrastructure
- **Deep localization**: Products entirely designed for Arab users, supporting Arabic NLP
- **2024 net profit exceeded $120 million**

## 7. Common ICT Analysis of MENA Internet Companies

### Cloud Service Provider Distribution

| Cloud Provider | Major Clients | Data Center Location |
|----------------|---------------|----------------------|
| AWS | Careem, Talabat, Yalla, Amazon.ae | Bahrain (Middle East) |
| Microsoft Azure | Noon (Payments), Shahid | UAE |
| Google Cloud | Careem (BigQuery) | UAE |

### Key Trends

1. **Multi-cloud strategy**: Leading companies commonly use 2–3 cloud providers to avoid single-vendor dependency
2. **Cloud-first**: Except for Yalla, all major companies choose not to build their own data centers
3. **AI-driven**: All companies deeply apply AI in areas such as recommendations, fraud detection, and demand forecasting
4. **Going global and China**: Noon enters the Chinese market; Talabat/Yalla focus on Gulf localization

## 8. My MENA Observations

*The following content is based on field research and industry exchanges.*

![Dubai city skyline](https://images.unsplash.com/photo-1512453979798-5ea266f8880c?w=800&q=80)
*Dubai, the headquarters hub for MENA internet companies*

The MENA internet market is a unique case where policy drive outweighs market drive. The Saudi PIF’s strong hand gives Shahid and Noon capital support unimaginable in other countries. And Yalla’s profitability miracle proves that when localization is done to the extreme, a company can thrive even without relying on fundraising.

![Riyadh, Saudi Arabia](https://images.unsplash.com/photo-1574717024653-61fd2cf4d44d?w=800&q=80)
*Saudi Vision 2030 drives rapid development of the digital entertainment industry*

Looking at Chinese companies going overseas, success stories in MENA are concentrated in gaming (like miHoYo, Tencent) and new energy retail (like Temu, SHEIN). Pure internet platforms still face high localization barriers. The dominant advantage of AWS and Azure also limits opportunities for Chinese cloud providers mainly to hybrid cloud scenarios.

---

## Key Takeaways

- **Market is policy-driven**: Saudi PIF is the largest source of capital; the rise of Shahid and Noon is essentially a sovereign wealth fund’s strategic deployment, not a purely market-driven phenomenon
- **AWS dominates the MENA cloud market**: Leading companies (Careem, Talabat, Yalla, Amazon.ae) all build their core architecture on AWS; opportunities for Chinese cloud providers are limited
- **Super apps are the common endgame**: Careem integrates ride-hailing + food delivery + payments, Noon builds an e-commerce + payment + instant delivery ecosystem; platformization is the evolutionary direction of MENA internet
- **Yalla’s profitability model deserves attention**: Focusing on Arab localization with 40%+ net margins, it is a rare example of “self-sustaining” internet business in MENA
- **Chinese overseas opportunities lie in gaming and new energy retail**: Pure internet platforms face high localization barriers; gaming (miHoYo) and e-commerce (SHEIN) are successful benchmarks
